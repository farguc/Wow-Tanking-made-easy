Tank Fundamentals
The core concepts you need to tank confidently — from managing threat to reading boss mechanics and leading pulls.
🎯
Threat & Aggro
Your first job is keeping mobs on you. Use your highest-threat ability immediately on pull — most specs have an AoE taunt or threat dump.
  • Tab-target and apply your threat-builder on all mobs in large pulls
  • If a DPS pulls aggro, taunt immediately — don't wait
  • Use your AoE kit to maintain threat on spread packs
  • Casters need ranged abilities to reach them — or LoS them to you
🛡️
Defensive Cooldowns
Don't hoard your defensives. A cooldown used slightly early is worth far more than one held too long.
  • Learn which boss abilities hit hardest — use a major CD there
  • Minor defensives (passives, short CDs) fill the gaps between majors
  • Communicate with your healer: "I'm using X now" helps them plan
  • On Tyrannical weeks: save a major CD for the final boss enrage phase
🗺️
Leading Pulls (M+)
As tank, you control the pace of the run. Pull too little and you waste time. Pull too much and you wipe.
  • Learn the dungeon's % route before leading a group
  • Use Keystone.guru to study routes offline
  • Communicate skips — not everyone knows what you're doing
  • Pull into your healer's CDs, not away from them
  • Position large packs near walls so ranged DPS can cleave without moving
🛑
Interrupts & CC
Tanking isn't just surviving hits — it's managing caster mobs. Bad interrupts cause wipes more than bad positioning.
  • Open the dungeon journal and check which mobs have must-kick casts
  • Call out interrupt assignments in voice or chat before the pull
  • Tanks should cover kicks when DPS are busy or have no kick
  • CC (stun, fear, knockback) can pause dangerous casts if your kick is on CD
  • Stagger CC: don't chain-CC unless you need breathing room
📍
Positioning
Where you stand is half the battle. Bad positioning costs your DPS uptime and your healer mana.
  • Face bosses away from the group to avoid cleaves hitting DPS
  • Stack mobs tightly for AoE — hug them with your back to a wall
  • Don't kite into melee-friendly zones (corners, pillars are fine)
  • Leave room between you and hazards — give yourself an escape path
🔄
Taunt Swapping
Some bosses apply stacking tank debuffs. If you see a stacking debuff on yourself, taunt-swap with your co-tank before it gets too high.
  • Common swap triggers: 3–5 stacks of a damage-taken debuff
  • Coordinate via voice or WeakAuras; addons like BigDebuffs help
  • In 5-man content, solo tanking is the norm — fewer swap situations
  • Always watch for "Tank" mechanics in the Dungeon Journal
🏆
Best Tank Specs — Midnight Season 1
  • Protection Paladin — S-tier all-rounder. Best mitigation, off-healing, and Blessings for your group. Excellent vs magic damage and debuffs.
  • Brewmaster Monk — S-tier for smoothing damage spikes via Stagger. Predictable damage intake, great utility. Requires precise CD management.
  • Vengeance Demon Hunter — S-tier solo and in M+. Exceptional mobility, Sigil CC toolkit, snap threat. Best self-sustain of any tank.
  • Protection Warrior — Best choice for learning tank fundamentals. Classic, straightforward, very readable defensive kit.
  • Blood Death Knight — Strong self-healing, excellent for progression. Less mobile but incredibly durable.
  • Guardian Druid — Consistent passive mitigation. Good for groups that value stability over flashy utility.
⚙️
Recommended Addons for New Tanks
  • Details! — Damage/interrupt/death tracking. Learn from your runs.
  • BigDebuffs — Shows important debuffs on your frame. Never miss a stacking tank debuff.
  • Plater Nameplates — Colour-codes enemies by interrupt status. Essential for managing large pulls.
  • WeakAuras — Build or import auras for your spec's cooldown tracking.
  • Larias's Checklist — In-game weekly tracker so you stay raid-ready.
  • Keystone.guru (website) — Plan your M+ route before zoning in.
